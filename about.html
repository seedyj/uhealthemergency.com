Current Situation

In moments demanding urgent care and treatment, a plethora of uncertainties often arises. Emergency medical professionals work tirelessly to swiftly attend to patients, yet the nature of such situations necessitates a patient-centered approach and collaborative effort. Despite their best efforts, there are instances where patients may be unable to communicate or move, or may simply be uncooperative. In such scenarios, depending on the severity, these circumstances can escalate, potentially leading to misdiagnosis or further harm to the patient. To ensure patients receive proper and efficient care, many require a means to convey their health information. However, for numerous individuals, having someone constantly present is impractical. If there were an innovation capable of autonomously displaying accurate medical information without relying on the presence of a caregiver, it would address both challenges effectively.

Our Idea

The proposed solution is a medical card that offers instantaneous access to individuals' medical records when they require medical care. This card aims to expedite the treatment process in both emergency situations and routine medical settings. It is particularly beneficial for individuals facing connectivity issues, lacking internet knowledge, or living below the poverty line. Currently, approximately 12.22% of Americans lack internet access, which effectively isolates them from their medical records. Moreover, elderly individuals often struggle to navigate the process of accessing their medical information. Additionally, medical facilities incur significant expenses, ranging from $15,000 to $70,000, to operate medical record systems. Implementing medical cards would not only reduce operational costs but also streamline the identification and treatment process, potentially saving lives. Ultimately, this innovation aims to ensure fair and equitable access to medical care for all individuals.
